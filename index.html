The Feed

The For You page on TikTok is a highly personalized feed of videos algorithmically curated to a user's interests

On TikTok's website they state

Recommendations are based on a number of factors, including things like:

• User interactions such as the videos you like or share, accounts you follow, comments you post, and content you create.

• Video information, which might include details like captions, sounds, and hashtags.

• Device and account settings like your language preference, country setting, and device type. These factors are included to make sure the system is optimized for performance, but they receive lower weight in the recommendation system relative to other data points we measure since users don't actively express these as preferences

Project

I initially found the location for this performance while playing a fool's version of tennis in the tall grass with a friend. We had originally planned to play in the courts, but were intimidated by the community of professional players already gathered. This brings us to the audio. Instead of our voices, mine instructive, theirs inquisitive; we hear the soft hypnotic hits of the nearby tennis court, the barking of a nearby dog, and the hearty chuckle of a man, not to mention the droning muffle of audio fuzz.

Illustrations were an afterthought while in post production of the documentation. They ultimately were made to serve the purpose of keeping the viewer in touch with what the participants are learning. Although still vague, they add a bit of insight and incorporate my usual practice of illustrating.

The questions and accompanying quotes seen in the video were taken from conversations with the participants after filming.

1. How have you found yourself experiencing joy in life right now?

2. What experiences with flowers have you had most recently?

The structure for this performance was highly conscious of the way we are living currently. By sanitizing the florals between each shoot and laying the sheets over 6 feet apart, we maintained social distancing practices. While masks ultimately were not used for the filmed clips for clarity while teaching, they were worn off camera.

Some of the tips for floral design I taught them included:

• Starting with a base of greens in widespread position with varying heights creating a visual flow with heavy focal flowers from left to right

• Using the varying types of filler flowers to add texture and spacing

• Using both the horizontal and vertical axis to create depth within the arrangement

•The deeper colour of the focal florals pull focus, as their name implies

•In contrast the filler flowers are all lighter in colour and mass, to create an aspect of accentuation

Background

The Feed

Social media platforms have caused a substantial change in how media is consumed. Feeds, and the content on them, hold power over people’s interests, beliefs, relationships and more. With hyper personalized algorithms, a feed becomes a reflection of the user.

TikTok has the most advanced recommendation algorithm of any platform. The service is able to quickly and accurately determine content which users find interesting and create a hyper personalized feed.
Users browse the For You page to view videos algorithmically curated to their interests.

On TikTok's website they state

Recommendations are based on a number of factors, including things like:

• User interactions such as the videos you like or share, accounts you follow, comments you post, and content you create.

• Video information, which might include details like captions, sounds, and hashtags.

• Device and account settings like your language preference, country setting, and device type. These factors are included to make sure the system is optimized for performance, but they receive lower weight in the recommendation system relative to other data points we measure since users don't actively express these as preferences

For more information on the algorithm watch this video

With these feeds, content is more siloed to specific niches and interests. The only content surfaced outside of the user’s interests are “sponsored” posts (advertising).

This project tried to interrupt the feeds by surfacing content, as sponsored posts, with no data points to track.
The posts contained original sounds, imagery, and lacked hashtags, captions or any other defining data points which could sway the algorithm’s ability to surface them.

Project

I was inspired by the works of Chris Burden and Stan Douglas' commercial interventions and wanted to adapt some of the ideas to the contemporary media consumption format on TikTok. To challenge what Burden called the "omnipotent stranglehold,” which broadcast television had on content distribution, but against TikTok’s recommendation algorithm. The content was more inspired by Stan Douglas’ mundane “Television Spots”. The medium of short form video and an endless stream of content, creates a much shorter attention span. The content had to contain some form of visual and audio interest in order to keep some of the audience engaged.

It costs around the price of a coffee to promote a video for one day. I chose 3 videos to promote. TikTok allows users to advertise to people of specific ages, genders, and interests or have the algorithm choose automatically.

60 second videos of colours shifting in minor hue and brightness values accompanied by low pitch droning audio.

The goal was to see how people would react and interact with the foriegn content on their feed.
